The author

Elite on the bar. Elite on the course.

Cal Stone is the rare athlete who refused to pick a lane. He has toed the line at nearly a hundred obstacle course races — including several world championship events, at every distance from the short and savage to the ultra — and done it while putting up numbers most dedicated lifters never touch: a squat near three times his bodyweight, a bench near twice it. Elite power and elite endurance almost never live in the same body. In Cal's, they do.

He didn't arrive there gifted. Cal's first obstacle race ended with a walk to the finish line, barely inside the cutoff. What changed everything wasn't talent or a coach or a supplement — it was a notebook. He wrote down every set, every mile, every honest number, and let the pages do their work: turning guesswork into progression, and progression, repeated for years, into results most people would call out of reach.

That's the whole idea behind The Train & Track Series. Every program is one Cal has run in his own training — plain English, no filler, no gimmicks — welded to the logbook habit that built him. Forty titles, one belief: the people who write it down are the people who go the distance.
